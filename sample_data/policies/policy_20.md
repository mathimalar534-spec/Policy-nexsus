# Monitoring Policy

**Version:** v3.2  
**Owner:** Liam Carter (Finance)  
**Last Reviewed:** 2025-07-31  
**Status:** Draft

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the monitoring policy for the organization. It is maintained by the Finance department and applies to all personnel and systems in scope below.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to the following groups: admins, all users, contractors, developers.

## 3. Obligations

### 3.1 Access

Access control obligations define who may reach which systems and data.

- **[Prohibited]** Developers prohibited access. (Reference: NIST SP 800-53 Rev 4)

### 3.2 Logging

Logging obligations define what activity must be recorded and for how long.

- **[Mandatory]** Admins must logging.

### 3.3 Mobile

Mobile device obligations apply to any device used to access company resources.

- **[Mandatory]** All Users shall mobile.
- **[Mandatory]** Admins required mobile.

### 3.4 Third-party

Third-party obligations govern engagements with vendors and external partners.

- **[Recommended]** Contractors should third-party.

### 3.5 Privacy

Privacy obligations govern the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data.

- **[Recommended]** All Users should privacy.

### 3.6 Asset

Asset management obligations cover the tracking and disposal of company assets.

- **[Recommended]** Admins recommended asset.

### 3.7 Provisioning

Provisioning obligations define how user accounts are created and deprovisioned.

- **[Recommended]** All Users should provisioning. (Reference: WEP)

### 3.8 Cloud

Cloud obligations apply to infrastructure and services hosted with cloud providers.

- **[Mandatory]** Developers required cloud.

### 3.9 HR

HR obligations apply to onboarding, offboarding, and personnel security practices.

- **[Mandatory]** Developers shall HR. (Reference: SHA-1)
- **[Recommended]** Contractors recommended HR.
- **[Mandatory]** Contractors required HR.
